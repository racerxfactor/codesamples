It takes the combined teamwork of the world's top broadcast, cable and digital talent to deliver the world's top sports entertainment to a global audience. At NBC Sports Group, hundreds of professionals of diverse backgrounds and expertise come together to deliver the very best in sports programming. In addition to our broadcast division, NBC Sports Group owns and operates two U.S. cable sports channels, a U.S. sports radio network, eight regional sports networks and multiple sports websites.

A World Of Opportunity

To cover the entire global span of sports, we put our top-notch talent in the thick of the action, from regional events to some of the most-watched events in the world. We attract the best professionals in the business and keep them in the NBC Sports family with the opportunity to work on programming that includes such prestigious events as 'NBC Sunday Night Football,' which is the premier primetime NFL game of the week; 'Football Night in America,' America's most-watched NFL studio program; Notre Dame college football; the Kentucky Derby and Preakness Stakes; the U.S. Open Championship, and NHL Stanley Cup Playoffs and Final.

A Proud Legacy

The history of NBC Sports is as long and storied as televised sports itself. Known as America's Olympic Network, NBC has broadcast 12 Olympic Games, more than any other network. Additionally NBC has broadcast 17 Super Bowls, 13 Kentucky Derby, 38 World Series, 43 Wimbledon Championships. Distinguished by its ability to amass large audiences with broad promotion, particularly the Olympics, NBC Sports has broadcast nine of the top 10 most-watched events in U.S. television history, including the 2008 Beijing Olympic Games, which stands at No. 1 all time with 215 million viewers.
